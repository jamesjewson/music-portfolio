Trumpet

James Jewson is a versatile musician with extensive experience in orchestral, jazz, chamber, and commercial music. He has studied with world class musicians, including members of the Chicago Symphony, Pittsburgh Symphony, and Minnesota Orchestra. Jewson has performed with the likes of Wycliffe Gordon, Philip Dizack, and John Hagstrom. In 2019, he served as lead trumpet for the Devonshire Cultural Center in Skokie, Illinois, including performances of musicals Follies and A Chorus Line. Jewson has performed in Chicago's prestegious Symphony Center, Auditorium Theater, and Studebaker Theater.

In addition to being an avid performer, while residing in Chicago Jewson maintained a private trumpet studio, and taught trumpet and horn at the prestegious Latin School of Chicago. He holds a Bachelor's Degree in Music Education and Trumpet Performance from UW-La Crosse, and a Master's Degree in Orchestral Trumpet from the Chicago College of the Performing Arts. He currently resides in Appleton, Wisconsin with his wife, Laura.

Eclectic Array

Eclectic Array is the pseudonym James writes under when creating non-classical music. Drawing inspiration from artists Haywyre, Emancipator, Tycho, and Christian Scott Atunde Adjuah (among others... the list is quite long), Eclectic Array is exactly what it claims to be- an eclectic mixture of electronic, lo-fi, classical, jazz, and world music.
